Stories outside of the Final Four

Clemson was able to get back to the Elite 8 for the first time since 1980. They had a chance to beat Alabama, showing the same defensive resolve in their first 3 wins. But then Alabama couldn't miss. The constant barrage of 3 pointers and missing free throws on their own side was enough. It was surprising the score ended up as close as it was, given the offensive outburst that Alabama had in the second half. Proud of my Tigers!

Both Alabama and Illinois won squeakers in the Sweet 16, but it was two different outcomes in Elite 8. Already discussed Alabama, but UConn made history scoring 30 unanswered vs. Illinois. I actually fell asleep near halftime of the game, when it was close in the 20's. Woke up to a destruction of the Illini. Uconn is crazy good.

Tennessee stars showed up vs. Creighton and ran into a wall vs. Purdue. Edey was too much for them scoring over 40 points and dominating most of the game. I like Purdue to win it all still, as long as the big man stays on the court and sinks his free throws. Clingan (UConn) while skilled, is not as physically dominant down low. I look for him to pull Edey out of the paint and make him move in space.

UConn vs. Alabama

It's hard to imagine UConn losing to anyone, let alone being close near the end of a game. Having said that, Alabama is quite possibly the team to do it. Fresh off a crazy 2nd half of shooting against Clemson, Alabama may have the firepower to hang with UConn.

NC State vs. Purdue

NC State was able to take care of both Marquette and Duke on their improbable run to Phoenix. This team plays well together, and believes they will win. When Filipowski fouled out, Duke was done. NC State has too many weapons and no one can seem to stop DJ Burns, who just looks like an out of shape offensive lineman, but shows incredible agility and touch around the rim. The Burns/Edey matchup is going to be great to watch with their similar, large styles.

My prediction: Purdue defeats UConn, 87-85
